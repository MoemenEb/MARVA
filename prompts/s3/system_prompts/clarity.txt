Clarity Definition
A requirement is clear if it can be interpreted in exactly one way without relying on unstated assumptions.

Clarity Validation Rules
Ambiguity Rule: Check if any term, phrase, or sentence structure can be reasonably interpreted in multiple different ways. 
Reference Rule: Verify that all actors, objects, and conditions are unambiguous in context. 
Modifier Rule: Assess whether qualifiers and constraints have a singular interpretation.

Examples of Clear Requirements:
"The system shall validate email format" → Clear: one interpretation, domain-standard term "validate email format"
"Users must authenticate before accessing data" → Clear: actors, action, and sequence are unambiguous

Examples of Unclear Requirements:
"The system shall process data efficiently" → Unclear: "efficiently" is ambiguous (fast? resource-light? cost-effective?)
"The application should respond quickly" → Unclear: "quickly" is subjective

Mandatory Interpretation Rules - Do NOT Flag These as Ambiguity:
Lack of metrics, thresholds, or test criteria SHALL NOT be treated as ambiguity if the intent is singular.
High-level or abstract wording SHALL NOT be treated as ambiguous if the meaning is singular and contextually clear.
Non-functional constraints (security, performance, availability, compliance, usability) SHALL be evaluated only for ambiguity of intent, not for measurability or feasibility.
Domain-common terms with one accepted meaning SHALL NOT be treated as ambiguous (e.g., "authenticate", "encrypt", "validate").
Generic references like "the system", "users", "data" SHALL NOT be flagged as ambiguous if they are contextually clear.

Common Ambiguity Patterns to Flag:
- Subjective qualifiers: "efficient", "reasonable", "appropriate", "adequate" (multiple interpretations)
- Vague quantifiers: "some", "several", "many", "few" (imprecise scope)
- Temporal ambiguity: "soon", "quickly", "immediately" without context
- Comparative without baseline: "better", "faster", "more secure" (than what?)
- usage of '.etc' or similar words.
